I'm Paulus


Every wall is a door

I had a friend once who's learned Spanish fluently all by himself. 'Przemek's a pro.' - I used to think. 'Unlike me... the embodiment of mediocrity... I only watch anime all day and don't do anything creative or productive.' And I used to live with a firm belief, that he's a badass, and I am just not - daydreaming what it'd be like to be born someone special, like Przemek. He obviously denied it and insisted that it's nothing special, but I just stubbornly persisted with my mindset for years.

At the time I used to call myself 'lazy bum' and 'toxic waste'. Even on many online forums, my 'about' section said exactly that 'lazy bum/toxic waste'. And with such a mindset I reluctantly started to learn Spanish myself with Pimsleur and Duolingo - not with a proper textbook, because textbooks were for the pros, but with something for a noob nonentity like me. And it turned out that when I've started with little things, I could proceed to the more complex ones, and with time even to understanding Spanish podcasts and books, and with hard work and persistence eventually even talk with real Spaniards... IN SPANISH! And much better than Przemek when I used to hail him a demigod. And then I realised, that I've reached a level that I previously perceived as badassdom, but I didn't feel like a badass at all.

And that realisation was an epiphany. There are no badasses, there are only people of hard work and iron will, who toil and sweat like everyone out there, with the only difference being that they don't give up. And they are perceived by the lazy as pros and badasses, even though in reality they are NOT ANY DIFFERENT. Every single one of them was at some point a lazy bum with no motivation, but he hitched himself up to a plough, grit his teeth, and ploughed the acreage he'd planned to plough... even if his hands bled afterwards.

But only after having done it are you able to realise that you can ACTUALLY do it.

And so I kept on doing it.

Now, apart from my native Polish language, I use English and German fluently, beginner-level French and can still fairly well read Spanish (after not having practised it for years).

I am an avid maker/diyer, who has to put his screwdriver and soldering iron into every piece of tech he buys.

I always work until I'm done and never back down from a challenge.

So if you're looking for someone resourceful, driven, conscientious, thinking beyond the box, highly tech literate, quickly learning, team-dedicated, well-organized, tolerant and compassionate...

you've just found him :)
